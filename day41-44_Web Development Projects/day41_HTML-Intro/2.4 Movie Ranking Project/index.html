The Best Movies According to Kiernan

My top 3 movies of all time

Interstellar

Nearly every movie produced by Christopher Noal is sure to be a great watch, eliciting a level of intellectual investment from the viewer, coupled with a suprising amount of visual artistry, all while avoiding CGI wherever possible. This story depicts a man grappling with the desires of his past, which were ripped away from him without potential for execution, until a new opportunity presents itself in the form of an unexpected visitor. Though the journey extends through decades and thousands of miles, the underlying message becomes apparent as one discerns whether the promise was fulfilled.


Arrival

If you knew the outcome of a situation, even if potentially traumatic, would you still walk the path? Unexpected visitors carry a message to Earth, triggering a race against nations across the globe to be the first to discover the meaning. Are these visitors friend or foe? Emotional connections tie the visitors to the researchers tasked with deciphering the messages, but the power of the messages conveyed may be more than anticipated, potentially even paradoxical.


The Matrix

As we venture closer toward a potential for intelligent design, the question about whether technology will reach the point of superceding human interlligence and potentially replacing itself above us in the social heirarchy, a programmer's world is ripped from underneath him after making a choice to know the truth. In order for the truth to be able to stand on its own, one must first remove knowledge of anything which contradicts, in the search for the one true meaning.
